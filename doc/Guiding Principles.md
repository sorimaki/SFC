# SFC — Story First Culture

# Guiding Principles

**Document Version:** 0.1
**Status:** Draft
**Last Updated:** 2026-07-12

---

# 1. Purpose of This Document

This document defines the fundamental principles that guide the design, development, and evolution of SFC.

These principles are intended to provide consistency when making product, technical, and business decisions.

When evaluating a new idea or feature, the first question should be:

> Does this support the fundamental purpose and values of SFC?

If the answer is no, the feature should be reconsidered, postponed, or rejected.

---

# 2. Story Comes First

## Principle

The story is the foundation of SFC.

Media exists to support the story, not the other way around.

## Explanation

SFC is not designed around uploading isolated pieces of media.

A photo, video, animation, or audio file gains meaning through context.

Creators should begin with:

> "What story do I want to tell?"

not:

> "What content can I upload?"

## Implication

Features should encourage:

* structure,
* context,
* narrative flow,
* meaningful communication.

Features that encourage disconnected content should be avoided.

---

# 3. Purpose Before Popularity

## Principle

SFC values meaningful storytelling over maximizing engagement metrics.

## Explanation

Many modern platforms optimize for:

* views,
* clicks,
* reactions,
* time spent.

SFC should avoid becoming a platform where content is created primarily to attract attention.

The goal is not:

> More content.

The goal is:

> Better stories.

## Implication

Success metrics should include:

* creator satisfaction,
* viewer understanding,
* meaningful interactions,
* quality of experiences.

---

# 4. Structure Before Randomness

## Principle

Stories require structure.

## Explanation

SFC content is organized through a hierarchy:

```text
Channel

    Series

        Season

            Episode

                Page

                    Story Component
```

This structure provides context and allows creators to build complete experiences.

## Implication

The platform should make structured storytelling easier than publishing disconnected media.

---

# 5. Channel Before Creator

## Principle

The Channel is the primary public identity.

## Explanation

Creators are important, but viewers primarily discover and follow publishers, topics, and storytelling identities.

Examples:

A viewer searching for news looks for:

```text
CNN
```

not:

```text
Warner Bros.
```

The creator relationship exists, but the Channel represents the public presence.

## Implication

The platform should prioritize:

* Channel discovery,
* Channel identity,
* Channel reputation,
* Channel organization.

---

# 6. Creator Accountability

## Principle

Creators should be responsible for the content they publish.

## Explanation

SFC intentionally avoids anonymous content creation.

Creators establish an accountable relationship with the platform.

Creator identity information:

* is collected for accountability,
* is protected,
* is not the public identity.

## Implication

SFC should encourage responsible publishing while protecting creator privacy.

---

# 7. Quality Over Quantity

## Principle

A smaller number of meaningful stories is preferable to an unlimited stream of low-value content.

## Explanation

SFC is not designed around content volume.

The platform should encourage creators to invest thought and purpose into their work.

## Implication

The platform should avoid mechanisms that reward:

* spam,
* repetitive uploads,
* meaningless engagement farming.

---

# 8. Simplicity Before Complexity

## Principle

The MVP should solve the core problem with the simplest practical solution.

## Explanation

Complexity should only be introduced when it creates meaningful user value.

A feature is not valuable because it is technically impressive.

It is valuable because it improves the storytelling experience.

## Implication

Every feature should answer:

> Does this help creators tell better stories?

If not, it should likely be postponed.

---

# 9. Creator Freedom Within a Structured Environment

## Principle

SFC should provide creative freedom without sacrificing organization.

## Explanation

Creators should be able to tell many types of stories:

* tutorials,
* fiction,
* travel experiences,
* education,
* personal stories,
* artistic works.

However, the platform provides structure to help creators communicate effectively.

## Implication

SFC should not restrict creativity.

Instead, it should provide powerful tools inside a meaningful framework.

---

# 10. Preserve Creator Intent

## Principle

The platform should preserve the creator's intended presentation.

## Explanation

A story is not only the individual components.

The relationship between components matters:

* position,
* timing,
* sequence,
* visual composition,
* audio,
* transitions.

Changing the presentation can change the meaning.

## Implication

The viewer experience should respect:

* the author's canvas,
* composition,
* timing,
* playback decisions.

---

# 11. Progressive Complexity

## Principle

SFC should support beginners without limiting advanced creators.

## Explanation

Different creators have different needs.

A traveler sharing vacation memories should not need professional animation skills.

An animator or professional creator may want advanced control.

## Implication

The platform should evolve toward:

### Simple Creator Experience

For:

* families,
* travelers,
* hobbyists,
* educators.

### Advanced Creator Experience

For:

* artists,
* animators,
* professional publishers.

---

# 12. Build the Foundation Before Expanding

## Principle

Core capabilities must be stable before adding advanced features.

## Explanation

Future possibilities include:

* collaboration,
* paid content,
* communities,
* AI tools,
* organizations,
* classrooms.

However, these depend on a strong foundation.

## Implication

The development priority should be:

1. Story creation
2. Story presentation
3. Viewer experience
4. Community features
5. Advanced capabilities

---

# 13. Avoid Becoming Another Social Media Feed

## Principle

SFC should not optimize for endless scrolling.

## Explanation

The platform exists to help people create and consume stories.

The goal is intentional discovery, not infinite consumption.

## Implication

The user experience should prioritize:

* searching,
* browsing,
* following,
* exploring topics,
* completing stories.

---

# 14. Design for Long-Term Trust

## Principle

Trust is more valuable than short-term growth.

## Explanation

SFC should prioritize:

* creator responsibility,
* transparent rules,
* respectful communities,
* quality experiences.

## Implication

Business decisions should consider long-term reputation, not only immediate growth.

---

# 15. Summary of Guiding Principles

SFC is guided by the following beliefs:

1. Story comes before media.
2. Purpose comes before popularity.
3. Structure comes before randomness.
4. Channels represent publishers.
5. Creators are accountable.
6. Quality matters more than quantity.
7. Simplicity comes before complexity.
8. Creativity should exist within meaningful structure.
9. Creator intent must be preserved.
10. Complexity should grow progressively.
11. The foundation comes before expansion.
12. SFC is a storytelling platform, not another social media feed.
